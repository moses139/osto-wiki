▶ Background

When TERA launched, its developers wanted a gearing system that rewarded progression without making equipment feel completely deterministic. In many MMOs, once a player obtained a piece of gear and upgraded it to its maximum enchant level, there was little reason to pursue that same item again.

To address this, the developers introduced small, randomized stat rolls within the gear's bonuses—1%, 2%, or 3% of a base stat. The difference between these rolls is intentionally small as the goal is not to drastically change player power, but to introduce subtle item quality variance that rewards players who continue optimizing their gear.

▶ Why This Tool Exists

TERA does not display these bonus percentages directly. Instead, it shows the final calculated stat values, which are derived from the base stat, enchant multipliers, and the hidden bonus percentage. Because the game also rounds values down during calculations, the resulting numbers can appear inconsistent, making it difficult to immediately determine whether a roll is +1%, +2%, or +3%.

For this reason, community-developed calculators were created to reverse-engineer the hidden percentage from the final stat values and accurately determine an item's bonus. Earlier tools, however, often required excessive manual input or relied on dense spreadsheet-style layouts that were difficult to parse at a glance.

In this vein, this tool was created to provide a clearer, more streamlined experience for an otherwise mysterious aspect of the game.

▶ How It Works

This tool calculates the quality of your gear bonus rolls using the following formulas:

Weapon:

Bonus% = ( AttackBonus − ROUNDDOWN( AttackMod × 0.03 × Enchant ) ) / AttackMod

Chest, Gloves, Boots:

Bonus% = ( DefenseBonus − ROUNDDOWN( DefenseMod × 0.045 × Enchant ) ) / DefenseMod

Roll Quality Interpretation:

  • 1% (Red) — Worst Roll (Bonus% < 0.01)
  • 2% (Orange) — Average Roll (Bonus% 0.01 to 0.025)
  • 3% (Green) — Perfect Roll (Bonus% ≥ 0.025)

The formulas compare your actual bonus to the minimum expected value for your enchant level, then turn that difference into a quality score.
